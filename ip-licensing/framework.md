# Squad IP Licensing Framework

## Overview

The Squad framework is published under a tiered licensing model designed to maximize open-source adoption while creating commercial pathways for organizations that deploy Squad in production at scale or as part of commercial products.

---

## Tier 1: Open Source (MIT License)

**License:** MIT  
**Scope:** Full framework source code, agent charter templates, routing logic, documentation

**Who this covers:**
- Individual developers and researchers using Squad for personal or exploratory projects
- Open-source projects and non-profit organizations
- Academic institutions using Squad for research or teaching
- Internal tools not distributed externally or sold commercially

**Conditions:**
- Attribution in source files and documentation (standard MIT requirement)
- No warranty; no liability for use

**What you can do:**
- Fork, modify, and redistribute the source code
- Build applications on top of Squad for internal use
- Contribute improvements back to the main repository

The full MIT license text is included in the repository's `LICENSE` file.

---

## Tier 2: Commercial License

**License:** Squad Commercial License v1.0  
**Target:** Companies deploying Squad in production as part of commercial software products or services

**Who this covers:**
- SaaS companies embedding Squad or Squad-derived orchestration logic in products sold to customers
- Consulting firms delivering Squad-based implementations to clients under commercial contracts
- Enterprises deploying Squad in production environments with revenue impact (>$500K ARR attributable to the deployment)

**What this enables beyond MIT:**
- White-label deployment: remove Squad branding and attribution requirements from end-user interfaces
- Priority support and SLA-backed response times
- Access to private pre-release builds and roadmap input
- Legal indemnification for commercial deployments

**Pricing:**
Commercial licenses are issued annually. Pricing is based on deployment scale:

| Deployment Scale | Annual License Fee |
|---|---|
| Startup (< $5M ARR) | $4,800/year |
| Growth (< $25M ARR) | $12,000/year |
| Scale (< $100M ARR) | $24,000/year |
| Enterprise (> $100M ARR) | Contact for pricing |

To inquire about a commercial license, contact the licensing team.

---

## Tier 3: Enterprise License

**License:** Squad Enterprise License (custom)  
**Target:** Large enterprises, regulated industries, government, and organizations with complex compliance requirements

**Who this covers:**
- Organizations in regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, defense) with specific compliance requirements
- Multi-entity deployments spanning subsidiaries, business units, or portfolio companies
- Organizations requiring on-premises deployment with air-gapped environments
- Strategic partners and OEM integrators

**What this includes:**
- All commercial license rights
- Unlimited deployment scope (no per-seat or per-instance limits)
- Custom SLAs and dedicated technical support
- Source code escrow arrangements available
- Co-development and roadmap influence for strategic partners
- Custom training and enablement programs
- Legal review and custom contract terms

Enterprise licenses are negotiated directly and may include multi-year commitments with corresponding pricing structures.

---

## Contribution and License Back

Open-source contributions to the Squad framework are welcomed under a Contributor License Agreement (CLA). By submitting a pull request, contributors grant the project maintainers the right to include the contribution in both the open-source MIT release and commercial license tiers.

This dual-licensing model is standard practice for commercially supported open-source projects (examples: MongoDB, Elastic, HashiCorp before their BSL transitions) and allows the project to sustain ongoing development while remaining freely available for non-commercial use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use Squad for internal tools at my company without a commercial license?**  
Yes. Internal tools not sold externally or embedded in commercial products fall under the MIT license, regardless of company size.

**Do I need a commercial license to offer Squad consulting services?**  
No. Consulting work (implementing Squad for a client, configuring agents, building integrations) does not require a commercial license. The license requirement applies when you embed Squad or Squad-derived logic into a product you sell.

**What counts as "white-label" deployment?**  
Any deployment where the end-user interface does not visibly reference Squad, the framework, or the originating project. If you're removing attribution to ship under your own brand, you need a commercial license.

**Can I get a trial of commercial features?**  
Yes. A 30-day evaluation period is available for teams assessing the commercial tier. Contact the licensing team to arrange access.
