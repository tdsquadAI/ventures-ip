# Standard Consulting Engagement Terms

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2025

These Standard Consulting Engagement Terms ("Terms") govern consulting engagements between the consulting party ("Consultant") and the client entity ("Client"). These Terms are incorporated by reference into any Statement of Work, proposal, or engagement letter that references them.

---

## 1. Engagement Scope

### 1.1 Statement of Work
Each engagement shall be governed by a mutually agreed Statement of Work ("SOW") that specifies: (a) the deliverables to be produced; (b) the timeline and milestones; (c) the fees and payment schedule; (d) any specific technical or process requirements. These Terms supplement and are incorporated into each SOW.

### 1.2 Changes to Scope
Either party may request changes to the SOW in writing. Changes that affect fees, timeline, or deliverables require a signed Change Order before work begins. The Consultant is not obligated to perform out-of-scope work without an executed Change Order.

### 1.3 Client Responsibilities
The Client shall: (a) provide timely access to systems, personnel, and information necessary for the engagement; (b) designate a primary point of contact with authority to make decisions; (c) review and provide feedback on deliverables within the timelines specified in the SOW. Delays caused by Client failure to fulfill these responsibilities may result in corresponding adjustments to timeline and fees.

---

## 2. Fees and Payment

### 2.1 Fee Structure
Fees are as specified in the applicable SOW. The Consultant's current rate card is incorporated by reference and available upon request.

### 2.2 Invoicing
Invoices are issued monthly for time-and-materials engagements, or per milestone for fixed-price engagements, as specified in the SOW. Invoices are due within 30 days of issuance.

### 2.3 Late Payment
Amounts unpaid after 30 days accrue interest at 1.5% per month (18% per annum), or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower.

### 2.4 Expenses
Out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the engagement (travel, lodging, third-party software required for deliverable production) are reimbursed at cost with prior written approval from the Client. Expenses exceeding $500 individually require pre-approval.

### 2.5 Taxes
Fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. The Client is responsible for all taxes associated with the engagement, excluding taxes on the Consultant's net income.

---

## 3. Intellectual Property

### 3.1 Work Product Ownership
Subject to full payment of all fees, and except as provided in Section 3.2, all deliverables produced specifically for the Client under an SOW ("Work Product") are assigned to the Client upon completion and full payment.

### 3.2 Pre-Existing IP
The Consultant retains all rights to: (a) intellectual property created prior to the engagement; (b) general methodologies, frameworks, tools, and know-how developed or refined during the engagement that are not specific to the Client's business ("Background IP"). The Consultant grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual license to use Background IP incorporated into Work Product for the Client's internal business purposes.

### 3.3 Open-Source Components
Where deliverables incorporate open-source components, such components remain subject to their original open-source licenses. The Consultant will identify all material open-source dependencies in deliverables.

### 3.4 Restrictions
The Client shall not sublicense, resell, or redistribute Work Product incorporating the Consultant's Background IP without prior written consent.

---

## 4. Confidentiality

### 4.1 Mutual NDA
Both parties acknowledge that, in the course of the engagement, each may receive confidential information belonging to the other. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed in connection with the engagement that is marked confidential or would reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information.

### 4.2 Obligations
Each party agrees to: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent; (c) use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of the engagement; (d) apply at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care.

### 4.3 Exceptions
Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this agreement; (b) was known to the receiving party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of the disclosing party's Confidential Information; (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, or court order (with prompt prior notice to the disclosing party where permitted).

### 4.4 Duration
Confidentiality obligations survive termination of the engagement for three (3) years, except for trade secrets, which are protected indefinitely.

## 5. Representations and Warranties

### 5.1 Consultant Warranties
The Consultant represents and warrants that: (a) it has the right to enter into this engagement and perform the services; (b) services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner; (c) Work Product will not, to the Consultant's knowledge, infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.

### 5.2 Disclaimer
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN SECTION 5.1, SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS." THE CONSULTANT DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

---

## 6. Limitation of Liability

### 6.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS OR DATA, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

### 6.2 Liability Cap
The Consultant's total aggregate liability arising from or related to an engagement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client under the applicable SOW in the three (3) months preceding the claim.

---

## 7. Termination

### 7.1 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate an engagement for convenience with 15 days' written notice. Upon termination, the Client shall pay for all work performed and expenses incurred through the termination date.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches these Terms or an SOW and fails to cure the breach within 10 business days of written notice.

### 7.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination: (a) each party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information; (b) IP assignment under Section 3.1 applies only to fully paid, completed deliverables; (c) the following sections survive termination: 3 (IP), 4 (Confidentiality), 6 (Liability), 8 (General).

---

## 8. General

### 8.1 Independent Contractor
The Consultant is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, or agent of the Client. The Consultant retains control over the means and methods of performing services.

### 8.2 Governing Law
These Terms are governed by the laws of the jurisdiction specified in the applicable SOW, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### 8.3 Dispute Resolution
The parties agree to attempt good-faith negotiation before initiating formal dispute resolution proceedings. If negotiation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of a mutually agreed arbitration body.

### 8.4 Entire Agreement
These Terms, together with the applicable SOW, constitute the entire agreement between the parties regarding the engagement and supersede all prior discussions and agreements.

### 8.5 Amendments
Amendments to these Terms require written agreement signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

### 8.6 Severability
If any provision of these Terms is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.
